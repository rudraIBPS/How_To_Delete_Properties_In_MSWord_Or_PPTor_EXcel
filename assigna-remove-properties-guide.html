Guide 01 · File Management

Remove File Properties
Before Uploading Your Assignment

Follow these steps to strip personal metadata from your file before submission — your identity and device information should never be embedded in what you upload.

⚠️

Important: Complete all steps on a PC or Laptop running Windows. This removes hidden metadata such as author name, device details, and edit history from your file before it is submitted.

Step-by-Step Instructions
1
💾
Step 1
Download the File to Your PC / Laptop
Locate the assignment file and save it locally to your computer. Note the folder — you will need to find it in the next step.
2
🖱️
Step 2
Right-Click on the Downloaded File
Navigate to the file in File Explorer. Right-click on it to open the context menu.
3
📋
Step 3
Select "Properties" from the Menu
Scroll to the bottom of the context menu and click Properties. A dialog box will open showing file details.
4
🗂️
Step 4
Go to the "Details" Tab
In the Properties window, click the Details tab. This displays all metadata — author, device, revision history, and more.
5
🔗
Step 5
Click "Remove Properties and Personal Information"
At the bottom of the Details tab, click the link "Remove Properties and Personal Information" to open the removal tool.
6
🛡️
Step 6
Select "Remove the Following Properties from This File"
Choose the option "Remove the following properties from this file" — not the copy option — so changes apply directly to the original file.
7
☑️
Step 7
Click "Select All"
Click Select All to tick every checkbox in the list — marking all metadata for removal without exception.
8
✅
Step 8
Click "OK" to Apply
Click OK to confirm. All properties are permanently removed. Your file is now clean and ready to upload.
🎉

All Done — Your File is Ready to Upload!

You have successfully removed all personal metadata. Proceed to upload through the designated portal. Contact your coordinator if you face any difficulties.

Guide 02 · Presentation Skills

How to Design a PowerPoint Presentation Efficiently

A well-designed presentation communicates ideas clearly and professionally. Follow these steps to plan, structure, and design slides that make a lasting impact.

💡

Pro Tip: Great presentations are simple, visual, and consistent. Avoid cluttering slides with too much text — let visuals and headlines do the talking, and use your spoken words for detail.

Step-by-Step Instructions
1
🎯
Step 1
Define Your Purpose and Audience
Before opening PowerPoint, ask: What is the goal? and Who is the audience? A client pitch needs a different tone than an internal report. Clarity of purpose shapes every design decision you make.
2
🗺️
Step 2
Outline Your Content First (Before Designing)
Write out the key points you want to cover before touching PowerPoint. Group them into a clear flow: Introduction → Context/Problem → Main Points → Conclusion/Call to Action. This prevents rebuilding slides after they are made.
3
🎨
Step 3
Choose a Professional Theme or Template
In PowerPoint go to Design → Themes and pick a clean theme. Or use a free template from slidesgo.com or canva.com. Stick to 2–3 colours only — one primary, one accent, one neutral background.
4
🔤
Step 4
Set Consistent Fonts — One for Headings, One for Body
Go to Design → Fonts. Use a bold display font for headings (e.g. Montserrat, Raleway) and a clean readable font for body (e.g. Calibri, Open Sans). Never use more than 2 font families in one deck.
5
📐
Step 5
Use the Slide Master for Global Consistency
Go to View → Slide Master to set fonts, colours, and logo placement across all slides globally. Add your logo in the Slide Master so it appears on every slide automatically — no manual copy-pasting needed.
6
📝
Step 6
Apply the One Idea Per Slide Rule
Each slide should communicate one clear idea. Use a short headline (max 8 words) and no more than 3–5 bullet points per slide. If a slide feels crowded, split it into two slides.
7
🖼️
Step 7
Replace Text with Visuals Where Possible
Use icons, charts, images, and infographics instead of paragraphs. Insert icons via Insert → Icons. For data, use the built-in Chart tool. Free images: unsplash.com or pexels.com.
8
✨
Step 8
Add Subtle Animations — Don't Overdo It
Use Fade or Appear animations to reveal bullet points progressively. Go to Animations → Add Animation. Avoid flashy effects like "Spin" or "Bounce" — they distract from your message.
9
🔍
Step 9
Proofread and Review in Slideshow Mode
Press F5 to run the full slideshow. Check spelling, alignment, and logical flow between slides. Ask a colleague to review before final submission.
10
💾
Step 10
Save in the Correct Format
Save as .pptx for editing. Export as PDF (File → Export → Create PDF) for sharing — this prevents formatting changes when opened on different devices.
📊

Your Presentation is Ready!

A consistent theme, clear structure, and minimal text will make your deck look polished and professional. Remember — great slides support your speech, they don't replace it.

Guide 03 · Academic Writing

How to Make Citations & References
Using Google Scholar & MS Word

Proper citation is essential in academic and professional work. This guide walks you through finding sources on Google Scholar and inserting formatted references automatically in Microsoft Word.

📌

Note: Always confirm which referencing style is required — APA, Harvard, MLA, or Chicago are most common. Your institution or client will specify the style. This guide uses APA 7th Edition as the example.

Step-by-Step Instructions
1
🔍
Step 1
Go to Google Scholar
Open your browser and navigate to scholar.google.com. This is a free academic search engine that indexes peer-reviewed papers, theses, books, and conference articles from reputable sources worldwide.
2
🔎
Step 2
Search for Your Topic Using Specific Keywords
Type specific keywords into the search bar — e.g., instead of "climate change", try "climate change effect on crop yield developing countries". Use the date filter in the left sidebar to find recent publications within the past 5 years.
3
📄
Step 3
Select a Relevant Source and Note Its Details
Click an article title to read its abstract. Confirm it is relevant to your work. Note the author name(s), publication year, journal name, volume, issue, and page numbers — all needed for your reference.
4
🗃️
Step 4
Use the "Cite" Button on Google Scholar
Below each search result, click the quotation mark icon ("Cite"). A popup displays the citation in APA, MLA, Chicago, Harvard, and Vancouver formats. Copy the citation in your required style directly from this popup.
5
📝
Step 5
Open Microsoft Word and Click the References Tab
Open your document in MS Word. Click the References tab in the top ribbon. This tab contains all the tools you need to manage in-text citations, footnotes, and your bibliography.
6
⚙️
Step 6
Set Your Citation Style in MS Word
In the References tab, find the Style dropdown (next to "Insert Citation"). Select your required style — e.g., APA. Word will now automatically format all citations and the reference list in that style.
7
➕
Step 7
Insert a New Source Using "Insert Citation"
Place your cursor at the point in the text where you want to cite. Click Insert Citation → Add New Source. Fill in the form with details from Step 3, then click OK. Word inserts the correctly formatted in-text citation automatically.
8
📚
Step 8
Generate Your Reference List / Bibliography
At the end of your document, click where you want the reference list to appear. In the References tab, click Bibliography → References. Word auto-generates the complete reference list from all your inserted citations.
9
🔄
Step 9
Update the Reference List After Adding More Citations
After adding new citations, right-click the bibliography at the end of your document and select "Update Field". Word refreshes the list to include all newly added sources automatically.
10
✅
Step 10
Review and Verify All References
Manually check every citation against the original source. Confirm that every in-text citation has a matching reference list entry and vice versa. Tools like Zotero or Mendeley can help manage large reference libraries.
📚

References Complete — Well-Cited Work Ahead!

Accurate citations build credibility and protect you from plagiarism. Always verify formatting against your institution's guidelines. For large projects, Zotero or Mendeley are excellent free reference managers.

Guide 04 · Freelancing

How to Format a Freelance Invoice
Professionally

A professional invoice ensures you get paid on time and protects you legally. Follow this guide to create clear, complete, and professional invoices for every client engagement.

⚖️

Legal Note: An invoice is a legal document. Ensure all details are accurate and complete. Keep a copy of every invoice you send. Consult a tax professional for invoicing rules specific to your country or region.

Step-by-Step Instructions
1
📄
Step 1
Choose Your Invoicing Tool
Create invoices in Microsoft Word, Excel, Google Docs, or free platforms like Invoice Ninja, Wave, or Zoho Invoice. For beginners, a Word or Google Docs template is the easiest starting point — search "invoice template" in the Google Docs template gallery.
2
🏷️
Step 2
Add Your Freelancer / Business Details at the Top
Include: Your Full Name or Business Name, Address, Email, Phone Number, and if applicable, your Tax ID / GST / VAT number. This is your "seller" information and must appear on every invoice you send.
3
🔢
Step 3
Add a Unique Invoice Number
Every invoice must have a unique Invoice Number for tracking purposes. Use a format like INV-001 or include the year: INV-2025-001. Numbers must be sequential and never repeated across different clients or projects.
4
📅
Step 4
Include Invoice Date and Payment Due Date
Add the Invoice Date (today's date) and a clear Due Date. Common terms are Net 7, Net 14, or Net 30 — payment is due 7, 14, or 30 days from the invoice date. State this clearly, e.g., "Payment due within 14 days."
5
👤
Step 5
Add the Client's Details ("Bill To" Section)
Add a "Bill To" section with the Client's Name or Company Name, Address, and Email. Ensure the name exactly matches whoever processes the payment — this prevents disputes or payment delays.
6
📋
Step 6
List Services in an Itemised Table
Create a table with columns: Description | Quantity / Hours | Rate | Amount. List each service separately. Example: "Blog Article Writing | 3 articles | £50 each | £150". Itemised invoices are far more professional and dispute-proof than lump-sum amounts.
7
🧮
Step 7
Calculate Subtotal, Tax, and Total Amount Due
Below the table add: Subtotal (sum of all items), Tax / VAT if applicable (state the percentage, e.g., 18% GST), and the Total Amount Due in bold. Always state the currency clearly (USD, GBP, INR, etc.).
8
🏦
Step 8
Add Your Payment Details
Include how you want to be paid: Bank Transfer (Account Name, Account Number, Sort Code / IFSC / IBAN / SWIFT), PayPal email, or UPI / Razorpay for Indian freelancers. The simpler you make payment, the faster you receive it.
9
📝
Step 9
Add Notes and Terms & Conditions
Include a brief notes section with: a late payment policy (e.g., "2% monthly fee on overdue amounts"), a thank you note, and any project-specific terms agreed with the client. This protects you legally if payment is delayed or disputed.
10
📤
Step 10
Export as PDF and Send to the Client
Always save as PDF (File → Export → PDF) before sending. Never send an editable Word or Excel file. Email with a professional subject line: "Invoice INV-001 – [Your Name / Service]". Keep a copy filed by date and client name for your tax records.
🧾

Invoice Ready — You're Set to Get Paid!

A complete, professional invoice builds trust with clients and speeds up payment. File all invoices by date and client for your tax records. Follow up politely if payment is not received by the due date.
